Hello and welcome to Tamriel Foundry’s Alchemy Guide! In this guide we cover the basic and advanced mechanics of Alchemy in The Elder Scrolls Online as well as some practical tips for how to get the most out of your crafting and gathering time!

UPDATE: This guide has been updated for ESO update 4 (patch 1.4) and can be considered current as of September, 2014.

 

Why Alchemy is Useful

Alchemy is used to create potions that will help you in combat. These potions can replenish resources, stats, make you immune to knockback and disabling effects, invisible, increase your speed and boost your damage and much more. But unlike foods and drinks, potions don't last very long. If you're looking for items that boost your resources or recovery rates, you should check out provisioning.

If you would like to get a quick primer on the basics, check out the video! - Rudrias
 

Profitability: High (Gathering)

Only the reagents are profitable, they sell for a steady 100 gold a piece (9/14/13) with some exceptions like Namira's Rot, which is harder to come by and used in a lot of potion crafting. Selling solvents or created potions isn't going to get you much. Most people can make their own potions because alchemy is one of the easiest crafting skills to level up. That's why there is more demand in resources (ingredients) than finished products (potions). Solvents are also cheaper because it's easier to come by and you only need 1 solvent / 3 reagents.

 

Creation & Discovery

Creating a potion requires at two or three reagents with a common effect and one solvent. The solvent controls the level and potency of the potion, while the reagents decide what type of potion you create. You need at least two reagents with the same effect to discover that effect. For example, if you use Bugloss with Blue Entoloma to make a potion you'll discover the 'Restore Health' effect on both reagents. Don't worry, all reagents have four effects so the chances of having at least one common effect are pretty good. They are also listed below if you don't feel like finding out yourself.

Solvents (Water)

Each potion you craft requires a solvent, which is essentially a vial of pure water. The higher level solvent you use, the higher level the potion you produce. You’ll find higher level solvents the further you progress through your alliance. One type of solvent isn’t restricted to just one zone. Water can be collected from any of the following sources:

  • Water skins: little pouches that you can find almost everywhere.
  • Pure water: water springs that can be found in any body of water.
  • Bottles: can also be found almost everywhere
  • Containers: small chance, mostly other materials (not recommended)

Leveling Tips

  • Focus on unlocking the four traits on all eighteen reagents, learning these effects will grant you extra experience.
  • Put skill points in 'Solvent Proficiency' first, allowing you to use the highest level of solvent for more experience.
  • Only use two reagents per solvent for leveling Alchemy. The difference in experience isn't worth using a third reagent.
  • Use reagents that are easy to come by. I recommend making potions with the common Water Hyacinth.
  • Water Hyacinths are the easiest reagent to farm. Just following a river will get you plenty.
  • Whilst farming Water Hyacinths you'll also find a lot of solvents because they share the same areas (rivers).
  • Save up Namira's Rot, only use them for learning the effects and for VR5 potions as they are very rare.
  • Farming Pristine Water or Filtered Water might prove more difficult. If so, just skip them and use lower tier solvents.
  • Farming high tier solvents is really easy as they are spread over an entire alliance and not just 2 zones.
  • If you have some skill points left, invest at least one point in Keen Eye. It really does help you spot ingredients.
  • I recommend using HarvestMap and HarvestMerge for those who intend do a lot of farming. (not necessary for leveling)
 

Useful Potions

Best potion for Stamina builds.

  • Panacea of Health: Cloud Mist, Bugloss, Columbine and Mountain Flower This is the best restoration potion available as it restores all resources. There is a weaker version that restores only 290 Health over 10 seconds instead of 406. This weaker version can be created by replacing Mountain Flower with Dragonthorn or Columbine with Lady's Smock. This is not recommended unless you run out of Mountain Flowers or Columbines.
  • Panacea of Spell Power: Cloud Mist, Cornflower, Lady's Smock and Water Hyacinth (Namira's Rot) This is the best potion for Magicka builds to increase their DPS and restore some Magicka. I recommend using Water Hyacinth instead of Namira's Rot. Water Hyacinth is much easier to get and Namira's Rot should be used for other potions as it's very rare.
  • Panacea of Weapon Power: Cloud Mist, Blessed Thistle, Dragonthorn and Wormwood/Water Hyacinth This is the best potion for Stamina builds to increase their DPs and restore some Stamina. You can use Wormwood or Water Hyacinths in this case because both are equally useful and relatively easy to come by.
 

Alchemy Stations

Alchemy stations are found in towns and cities and are identified by a herb-like symbol on banners outside of the station and as an icon on the map.

The station itself resembles a wooden bench complete with liquids separating, potions brewing, and empty vials ready to be filled. Simply walk up to the station and press the interact key to get the crafting interface.

 

Skills

The skills for Alchemy that you can place points in to enhance the craft include Solvent Proficiency which is the only *required* skill to make end-game potions. However there are many other skills that improve and enhance the profession!

Unlike the armor professions, there really is no 'Best Order' of skills to allocate in alchemy. Keen Eye is important is you have trouble gathering, but most plants are rather obvious.
